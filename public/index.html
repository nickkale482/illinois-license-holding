Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Illinois real estate license holding company?
A license holding company allows Illinois-licensed real estate brokers to keep their license active without being affiliated with a full-service brokerage. Instead of paying MLS fees and association dues, agents pay a small annual fee and can still earn referral commissions.
How much does it cost to hold my Illinois real estate license?
Illinois Real Estate License Holding Company charges just $109 per year — with no MLS fees, no association dues, and no other hidden costs.
Can I still earn money with a held license?
Yes. You can refer buyers and sellers to any licensed broker you choose and earn 80% of the referral commission when the transaction closes.
What happens if my Illinois real estate license expires?
Once expired, you must retake education requirements and pay reinstatement fees to become active again. Keeping your license active through a holding company is far less expensive than letting it lapse.

What is the difference between a holding company and a full-service brokerage?
A full-service brokerage requires agents to pay MLS dues, association fees, and desk fees in exchange for full brokerage support. A holding company simply keeps your license active at minimal cost — ideal for agents not actively selling.
Can I upgrade from the holding company to a full brokerage?
Yes. Many agents who hold their license with us eventually join Kale Realty as full active agents when they are ready to sell again. Visit joinkale.com to learn more.
